# Video Prompting Mastery Guide

Transform your ideas into stunning videos with these advanced prompting techniques.

## üé¨ The Science of Perfect Video Prompts

### The 7-Element Framework

Every great video prompt contains these elements:

1. **Scene Description**: What's happening visually
2. **Style Direction**: The aesthetic and mood
3. **Camera Movement**: How the camera behaves
4. **Lighting**: The atmosphere and time of day
5. **Subject Focus**: Main elements and their actions
6. **Duration & Pacing**: Timing and rhythm
7. **Audio Direction**: Music and sound effects

## üéØ Advanced Prompting Techniques

### Emotional Storytelling

#### The Hero's Journey Format

```
Act 1 - Setup (0-20s): Establish character and world
Act 2 - Conflict (20-40s): Present challenge or problem
Act 3 - Resolution (40-60s): Show transformation or success
```

Example:

```
Create an emotional brand story following a small business owner.
Act 1: Early morning, empty bakery, owner preparing alone.
Act 2: Customers arriving, community gathering, business growing.
Act 3: Packed bakery, team working together, owner smiling proudly.
Style: Warm, documentary feel with handheld camera.
Music: Soft piano building to uplifting orchestral.
Duration: 60 seconds.
```

### Technical Specifications

#### Camera Movements Glossary

- **Dolly**: Camera moves toward/away from subject
- **Tracking**: Camera moves parallel to action
- **Pan**: Camera rotates horizontally
- **Tilt**: Camera rotates vertically
- **Crane**: Camera moves up/down dramatically
- **Orbit**: Camera circles around subject
- **Zoom**: Lens focal length changes

#### Lighting Moods

- **High Key**: Bright, minimal shadows (comedy, corporate)
- **Low Key**: Dark, dramatic shadows (thriller, drama)
- **Natural**: Soft, realistic lighting (documentary)
- **Neon**: Vibrant colored lights (nightlife, cyberpunk)
- **Golden Hour**: Warm, soft sunset lighting (romantic)

### Style References

#### Popular Aesthetic Styles

```
"Wes Anderson style": Symmetrical framing, pastel colors, whimsical
"Christopher Nolan style": Dark, complex, IMAX-worthy shots
"Studio Ghibli style": Anime-inspired, detailed backgrounds, magical
"Marvel style": Action-packed, CGI-heavy, dramatic angles
"Black Mirror style": Dystopian, tech-focused, unsettling
```

## üí° Troubleshooting Common Issues

### Problem: Videos Look Generic

**Solution**: Add unique details

```
Instead of: "Person walking in city"
Try: "Young entrepreneur in navy suit walking confidently through
Times Square at golden hour, newspapers flying, yellow cabs passing"
```

### Problem: Inconsistent Style

**Solution**: Use style anchors

```
Add: "Maintain consistent visual style throughout: [specific style]"
Example: "Maintain Pixar animation style throughout all scenes"
```

### Problem: Poor Pacing

**Solution**: Specify shot durations

```
Structure your prompt with timing:
"Opening (0-5s): Wide establishing shot
Development (5-20s): Medium shots with movement
Climax (20-25s): Close-up emotional moment
Resolution (25-30s): Pull back to wide shot"
```
